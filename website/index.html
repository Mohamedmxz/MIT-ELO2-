ELO2: Climate Prediction in Sudan

Exploring temperature and rainfall trends to support climate understanding using data science.

Data and Methods

This project uses the NASA POWER database to collect climate data for Sudan, focusing on surface temperature and total corrected precipitation (rainfall).

Sudan is divided into five regions for analysis: North, Central, East, West, and South. Data for each region are collected, cleaned, and merged into structured datasets suitable for machine learning modeling.

Only temperature and rainfall are included in this version, while other climate variables like humidity, wind, or pressure could improve accuracy in future iterations.

Findings / Insights

  • Temperature follows a steady seasonal pattern, making predictions relatively reliable.
  • Rainfall is more irregular, so predictions vary between regions and some months are harder to forecast accurately.
  • Forecasts are intended as guidance to understand trends, rather than exact predictions for operational planning.

Regional Insights

  • Rainfall variability: Southern and Western regions show more fluctuation, while the North remains mostly dry and stable.
  • Temperature trends: Temperatures are relatively stable but slightly increasing in recent years.
  • Inverse relationships: Most regions (Central, East, South, West) show negative correlations between rainfall and temperature.
  • Northern region anomaly: Slight positive correlation due to very low rainfall.
  • Regional contrasts: South has the strongest negative correlation (~ -0.55), showing a trade-off between temperature and rainfall intensity.
